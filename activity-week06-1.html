About Our Automotive Museum

One of North America's Largest Ferrari Collections

The Marconi is located in the heart of Orange County and houses a 70 plus car collection that is open to the public with a suggested $20 donation. Your donation goes directly to helping children in need.

We are proud to house a collection of high performance street cars and race cars including (but not limited to) Lamborghini's, Ferrari's, and American muscle cars. The open wheel car collection features cars driven by Founder Dick Marconi, his son John, and wife Bo. Additionally, there are many famous cars driven by famous drivers. A collection you won't want to miss.

View Museum Hours

Dick and John Marconi donated their car collection and the building that housed it to found the Marconi Automotive Museum & Foundation for Kids in 1994. We were voted SoCal's favorite museum!

After Dick had achieved success both personally and professionally, he wanted to give back to the community that helped build him. Creating the Foundation was the last chapter in his life mantra, "laern, earn, and return." The museum continues to raise money for at-risk children's charities throughout Orange County from visitors touring the collection and booked events.

Audit Summary Spreadsheet

An ARC Toolkit and Google Chrome Vox Screenreader accessibility audit of the original Marconi Museum website revealed issues with keyboard functionality, skip navigation, focus indication, as well as various links and labels lacking sufficiently descriptive information. In my own markup of the Marconi Automotive Museum Website, I solved several of the original website's issues with keyboard functionality, skip navigation, and focus indication, but my website was lacking in a few areas. More specifically, I needed to improve on making my accessibility buttons more distinguishable, making the contact and location information a bit more descriptive, as well as adding labels to my social media icons. I solved these issued by adding ARIA labels to both my accessibility icons as well as my social media icons, and I slightly tweaked the wording in the contact and location information to make the information more descriptive for all users.
